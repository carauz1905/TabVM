Essence

TabVM makes a local virtual machine behave like a browser tab — startable, inspectable, and closable without leaving your workspace.

Attributes
  • Precise — engineered, not decorative.
  • Local — it runs on your machine, bound to localhost.
  • Developer-first — terminal-literate, honest, auditable.
  • Calm — one accent, lots of quiet, no theatrics.
One-line positioning

Local VMs, managed like tabs — in your browser, on your machine.

01 · Foundations

The concept

The mark is not a metaphor bolted on afterward. It is the product drawn literally: a running VM console, sitting inside a tab.

Three parts, one story
  • The panel — an outlined window. The VM console the student works in.
  • The tab — the solid accent shape on top. The hero: this VM lives in a tab, not a separate OS window.
  • The prompt ›_ — a terminal cursor inside. Proof the machine is live and interactive, not a static picture.

Read together: a live VM session, running inside a tab. That is the entire product in one glyph — which is exactly why it does not look generic. It could not belong to any other tool.

Logo anatomy

The logo is a mark plus a wordmark. The wordmark splits the name into a written part and a machine part.

Mark

Pure geometry — renders identically everywhere. Carries the single accent color.

“Tab” — display

Set in Space Grotesk 600. The human-readable, spoken half of the name.

“VM” — mono

Set in JetBrains Mono 500. The machine half — a deliberate typographic gear-shift.

Misuse

The mark stays disciplined. These are the ways it breaks — and they are the fastest path to looking generic.

✕ Don’t recolor. One accent only.
✕ Don’t stretch or distort.
✕ Don’t place on low-contrast color.
✕ Don’t rotate or tilt.
✕ No shadows, bevels, or glows.
TabVM
✕ Don’t swap the type roles. “Tab” is display, “VM” is mono — never the reverse.

03 · Color

Usage & accessibility

The accent shifts by theme so contrast holds. Verified against WCAG 2.1.

PairingRatioVerdictUse for
#0E1116 on #FFFFFF~18:1AAABody text, light theme
#F0F3F6 on #0E1116~16:1AAABody text, dark theme
#0B7C6F on #FFFFFF~5.0:1AASmall accent text, light
#0E9E8E on #FFFFFF~3.3:1AA-largeLarge text, icons, UI marks only
#2FE3CE on #0E1116~11.5:1AAAAccent text & marks, dark
Do
  • Use Signal 700 (#0B7C6F) for small accent text on light — it clears AA.
  • Use Signal 600 for the mark, links, and large text on light.
  • Use Signal 400 for anything accent on dark.
  • Keep accent to roughly <10% of any surface.
Don’t
  • Set small body text in Signal 600 on white — it fails AA (3.3:1).
  • Fill whole panels or backgrounds with the accent.
  • Introduce a second accent hue to “add energy”.
  • Rely on color alone to signal VM state — pair it with a label or icon.

Typography

Two open-source faces, chosen so the name reads as two halves: the human and the machine.

Display · Space Grotesk
Aa Tab

Weights 400 / 500 / 600. Used for headings, the “Tab” in the wordmark, and product UI titles. Geometric with just enough character to avoid the default-sans look.

Mono · JetBrains Mono
Aa VM

Weights 400 / 500. Used for the “VM”, labels, hex values, code, VM IDs, ports, and terminal output. It is the voice of the machine.

05 · Motion

Motion

One motion idea owns the brand: the terminal caret. It signals a live machine — the same thing TabVM delivers.

Principles
  • Assemble once. On load: the tab drops in, the panel draws, the prompt appears — in ~0.6s, then it rests.
  • Then the caret lives. A steady 1.05s blink. Nothing else moves.
  • Ease, don’t bounce. cubic-bezier(.2,.7,.2,1). No overshoot, no spring.
  • Respect the user. Under prefers-reduced-motion, everything renders static and complete.

06 · In the wild

Voice & applications

Voice & tone
  • Precise over friendly. Say the exact action: “Start VM”, not “Let’s go!”.
  • Honest about the machine. Surface ports, states, and errors plainly.
  • Local & safe. Reassure without alarm: “Bound to localhost.”
  • Lowercase, technical. Command-like microcopy: start · stop · reset · open console.
